> 
> Natalia Coelho's excellent analysis of Mythos' capabilities based on publicly available information (linked in the quoted tweet) includes the below graph regarding the two models' performances on UK AISI's TLO benchmark.
> 
> We already knew that GPT-5.5 performed slightly better on this benchmark than Mythos on average (although completely succeeding on it in only 2/10 attempts vs. 3/10 for Mythos), but it came as a surprise to me that GPT-5.5's attempts cost only ~$500 on average, compared to Mythos' ~$2.6K.
> 
> If you are a hacker, you will not, ofc, care about the number of tokens your attack used.  The only factor you *would* care about (maybe) is the cost.  And I venture to guess that spending 500M tokens on GPT-5.5 vs. 100M tokens on Mythos would likely be a no-brainer (GPT-5.5 would win).
> 
> From Noam Brown's tweet from a few days ago about GPT-5.5's performance on TLO (linked below):
> 
> "After 100 million tokens, performance was still going up. What we're seeing here is not the capability ceiling."
